Still not convinced? Read some stories from coordinators, volunteers and the people we serve:


“Serving people who live on the streets and in shelters helps me see through the eyes of Jesus. Helping someone doesn’t always mean meeting their physical needs but showing them to a Father who loves them.”

Richard Bolland, Coordinator

“Building genuine friendships and walking a journey with some of the residents has been such an eye opening experience. You witness both the struggles and the breakthroughs and it totally challenges your perspective. You come to realise just how privileged we really are.”

Matt Krog

“Volunteering at New Hope has really made me realise the impact and importance of relationships. I would arrive on a Tuesday thinking that we would just share food and talk about God but I would leave having had some of the best conversations with some of the most amazing people. Someone on the street once told me how they would rather know Jesus, have the amazing friendships they have now and be homeless than have all the money in the world. It really puts things into perspective.”

Neenah Moore
